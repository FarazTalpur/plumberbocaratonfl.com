Frequently Asked Questions

A backflow prevention device is a plumbing mechanism designed to prevent contaminated water from flowing backwards into the clean water supply in a water system.

Installing a backflow prevention device is crucial to safeguarding public health by ensuring that contaminated water does not mix with the potable water supply, thus preventing the spread of waterborne diseases.

Common types of backflow prevention devices include the reduced pressure zone (RPZ) valve, pressure vacuum breakers (PVB), and double check valve assemblies (DCVA).

Most local authorities require backflow prevention devices to be tested annually by a certified professional to ensure they function properly and comply with safety regulations.

Backflow prevention devices should be installed by licensed plumbers or certified backflow prevention device installers who have specific training and experience in handling such equipment.
