Frequently Asked Questions

A plumbing system inspection typically includes checking the condition of pipes, identifying leaks, assessing water pressure, examining fixtures and appliances, inspecting the water heater, and ensuring the overall system complies with safety standards.

It is recommended to have your plumbing system inspected at least once a year. However, older homes or systems with frequent issues may require more frequent inspections.

Common signs include low water pressure, frequent clogs, unusual odors, discolored water, inconsistent hot water supply, and increased water bills without changes in usage.

While homeowners can conduct basic checks for leaks or visible damage, a professional plumbing inspection is advised to thoroughly assess hidden issues and ensure all systems are functioning correctly and safely.

Regular plumbing inspections help identify and address small issues before they become major problems, extend the life of your plumbing system, improve safety, and can potentially reduce water bills by identifying leaks.
